译者马文韬，北京大学外国语学院德语系教授，2001年1月15日获奥地利共和国政府颁发的“杰出翻译奖”。<br>

Erich Fried按照“德汉词典”和新华社的“德语人名译名手册”应该译作“埃里希·弗里德”。<br>

<blockquote>

  <h3><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">1. What It Is<br>

  </span></h3>

  <p><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">It is madness<br>

  says reason<br>

  It is what it is <br>

  says love<br>

  <br>

  It is unhappiness<br>

  says caution<br>

  It is nothing but pain<br>

  says fear<br>

  It has no future<br>

  says insight<br>

  It is what it is<br>

  says love<br>

  <br>

  It is ridiculous<br>

  says pride<br>

  It is foolish<br>

  says caution<br>

  It is impossible<br>

  says experience<br>

  It is what it is<br>

  says love.</span></p>

  <h3><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">2. Bush with Heart-shaped Leaves<br>

  </span></h3>

  <p><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">Warm summer rain:<br>

  When a heavy drop falls<br>

  the whole leaf quivers.<br>

  So my heart quivers<br>

  each time your name falls on it.<br>

  </span></p>

  <h3><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">3. But</span></h3>

  <p><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt"><br>

  At first I fell in love<br>

  with the brightness of your eyes<br>

  with your laugh<br>

  with your joy in life<br>

  <br>

  Now I love your weeping too<br>

  and your fear of life<br>

  and the helplessness<br>

  in your eyes<br>

  <br>

  But I will help you<br>

  with your fear<br>

  for my joy in life<br>

  is still the brightness of your eyes<br>

  </span></p>

  <h3><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">4. Without You<br>

  </span></h3>

  <p><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">Not nothing<br>

  without you<br>

  but not the same<br>

  <br>

  Not nothing<br>

  without you<br>

  but perhaps less<br>

  <br>

  Not nothing<br>

  but less<br>

  and less<br>

  <br>

  Perhaps not nothing<br>

  without you<br>

  but not much more<br>

  </span></p>

  <h3><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">5. Humorless<br>

  </span></h3>

  <p><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">The boys<br>

  throw<br>

  in jest<br>

  (with) stones<br>

  at frogs<br>

  <br>

  The frogs<br>

  die<br>

  in earnest<br>

  </span></p>

  <h3><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">6. An Attempt</span></h3>

  <p><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt"><br>

  I have attempted<br>

  - while working -<br>

  to try<br>

  thinking of my work<br>

  and not of you<br>

  <br>

  And I am happy<br>

  that the attempt<br>

  did not succeed.<br>

  </span></p>

  <h3><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">7. Cancellation</span></h3>

  <p><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">Being able to breathe out<br>

  one's unhappiness<br>

  <br>

  breathe out deeply<br>

  so that one can<br>

  breathe in again<br>

  <br>

  And perhaps also being able to speak<br>

  one's unhappiness<br>

  in words<br>

  in real words<br>

  which are coherent<br>

  and make sense<br>

  and which one can<br>

  understand oneself<br>

  and which perhaps<br>

  someone else can understand<br>

  or could understand<br>

  <br>

  And being able to try<br>

  <br>

  That again would<br>

  almost be<br>

  happiness<br>

  </span></p>

  <h3><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt">8. Perhaps</span></h3>

  <p><span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt"><br>

  Remembering<br>

  that is<br>

  perhaps<br>

  the most painful way<br>

  of forgetting<br>

  and perhaps<br>

  the kindest way<br>

  of easing<br>

  this pain</span></p>

</blockquote>
