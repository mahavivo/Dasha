果然尤物啊!让驴儿开工！

果然尤物啊!让驴儿开工！还请善自珍摄，保重龙体。

前几天写一说茶水贴，恬示兄座，娱乐一下：


【‘tea’音小笺】

顷见范家材先生说Casino为闽南话“开始了”音译，如此趣闻，未知何据。我为潮汕人，家乡话与闽南话同出一源，不至太隔膜。大概闽南话“开始了”同样重音落在第二音节，酷似Casino，如果后者真是渊源于此，自是一“佳话”。于是想到英语中另一个来自闽南话的字眼：tea（茶），也颇有趣，请为谈助。

tea今读/ti:/，而“茶”在闽南话中，想必也和我家乡话一样，读/dē/，两者读音迥不相属，因而一直悬揣tea最初的读音可能为/tε&#601;/。后来读到Alexander Pope常被引作Zeugma例子的Couplet：

Here thou, great Anna! whom three realms obey,
Dost sometimes counsel take--and sometimes tea.

乃知tea读/tei/，与obey相对为韵。今查OED，tea旧或作tay，可见读音确为/tei/。又OED引及有荷兰语作te, thee者，明言其源自Amoy（约翰逊词典则说Tea was first imported from Holland by the earls of Arlington and Ossory, in 1666; from their ladies the women of quality learned its use），则te, thee之发音，似/dē/无疑。
Tea在17世纪进入英语时本读/tei/，那么又是什么时候音讹为/ti:/的呢？既然那时候不兴录音，这个问题一时恐怕无从精确考证。不过借助诗韵，庶几可以见出端倪。
这里仍要借重我们的蒲伯（1688-1744），因为他是茶的忠实粉丝。但既然茶在英国最先是由贵妇人领导潮流，权威自然是她们，那就ladies first，先看看两位女诗人的诗句：

Anne Finch, Countess Of Winchilsea (1661-1720)：

I hate such parts as we have plaid today,
Before I promis'd, had I read the play,
I wou'd have staid at home, and drank my Tea.
（AN EPILOGUE TO THE TRAGEDY OF JANE SHORE）

Lady Mary Wortley Montagu (1689-1762)（约翰逊词典中引了这几行，文字有出入，未详孰是）：

Why will Delia thus retire,
  And languish life away?
Why the sighing crows admire,
  'Tis too soon for hartshorn tea.
（SONG: 'WHY WILL DELIA THUS RETIRE'）

可见tea音/tei/是毫无疑义的。而在蒲伯的诗中，tea读/tei/的音例至少还有：

Soft yielding minds to water glide away,
And sip, with Nymphs, their elemental tea.
（THE RAPE OF THE LOCK）

Tell, tell your griefs; attentive will I stay,
Though time is precious, and I want some tea.
（THE BASSET-TABLE）

不过，在蒲伯的诗中，却又有tea音/ti:/者：

The snuff-box to Cardelia I decree.
Now leave complaining, and begin your tea.
（THE BASSET-TABLE）

To part her time 'twixt reading and bohea,
To muse, and spill her solitary tea;
（EPISTLE TO MRS TERESA BLOUNT）

这两处tea分别与decree、bohea（“武夷”茶，当然是闽南话！）押韵。尤其值得注意的是在同一首诗The Basset-Table中，tea与decree、stay分别押韵，可见在蒲伯的时代，tea并读/tei/、/ti:/，尚未统一，这是可以确定的结论。
Tea的读音什么时候统一为/ti:/，殆不可知。也许Great Vowel Shift余波所及，对tea也产生了影响。至迟在19世纪，tea可能已通读/ti:/，为了验证这个猜测，蒲伯之后，选择拜伦为考察对象。所得结果如下：

At least a quarter it can hardly be:
I dare say that his wife is still at tea.
（THE VISION OF JUDGMENT）

I can't say that she gave them any tea,
But there were eggs, fruit, coffee, bread, fish, honey,
With Scio wine, -- and all for love, not money.
（DON JUAN）

Here I must leave him, for I grow pathetic,
Moved by the Chinese nymph of tears, green tea!
Than whom Cassandra was not more prophetic;
For if my pure libations exceed tree,
I feel my heart become so sympathetic,
That I must have recourse to black Bohea:
'Tis pity wine should be so deleterious,
For tea and coffee leave us much more serious,
（DON JUAN）

Oh! ye, who make the fortunes of all books!
Benign Ceruleans of the second sex!
Who advertise new poems by your looks,
Your "imprimatur" will ye not annex?
What! Must I go to the oblivious cooks,
Those Cornish plunderers of Parnassian wrecks?
Ah! Must I then the only minstrel be,
Proscribed from tasting your Castalian tea?
（DON JUAN）

And when he walk'd down into the saloon,
He sate him pensive o'er a dish of tea,
Which he perhaps had not discover'd soon,
Had it not happen'd scalding hot to be,
Which made him have recourse unto his spoon;
So much distrait he was, that all could see
That something was the matter Adeline
The first but what she could not well divine.
（DON JUAN）

在拜伦的诗中，tea均读/ti:/。光凭拜伦这几个例子，当然还不能断定tea在19世纪已统一读/ti:/；若说tea在19世纪一般读音为/ti:/，应该是比较保险的。
也许可以说，tea读/ti:/是本土化的必然结果。茶的普通话音译cha进入英语并不比tea晚，却始终默默无闻，说不定就因为出口入耳皆不舒畅又没有改造的余地呢！
