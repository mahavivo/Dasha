Dog Latin
The phrase dog Latin refers to the creation of a a phrase or jargon in imitation of
 Latin, often by directly translating English words into Latin without conjugation
 or declension. 
One well-known use of dog Latin is in the temporary naming of undiscovered
 (or not yet officially named) chemical elements. For example, the name given to
 element 118 is "ununoctium", the IUPAC systematic element name, from the Latin
 words for "one one eight". 
Dog Latin, despite the similar name, is not related to Pig Latin. 



在拉丁语里本身没有J,比如大家经常见到的Jus(法律)其实在拉丁语里写做ius,所以此处的Jam当是iam.


拉丁语变尾非常复杂，在下只是略懂皮毛，给出的句子太短，不容易根据语境确定具体含义，下面给出各个单词可能的英语翻译，供参考：


iam : moreover, henceforth, indeed, just, further. 
iam : now, by now, already/ presently, immediately, soon. 


incipio , cepi, ceptum, 3 (archaic incepsit inceperit, Paul. ex Fest. p. 107 Müll.),
 v. a. and n. [in-capio; lit., to seize upon, lay hold of; opp. to desinere; hence, 
with the accessory idea of action), to begin to do something, to take in hand (syn. 
incoho; in class. prose, viz. in Cic., only in the tempp. press., while coepi is 
used in the tempp. perff.); constr. usually with the inf., less freq. absol., 
with the acc., ab, or adv. of place or time.

西班牙语 incipiente:开始的，刚出现的，早期的。


sedeo , sedi, sessum, 2, v. n. [Sanscr. root sad-; Gr. HeD, to sit; cf. e+dos, e+zomai; 
Lat. sedes, insidiae, sedare, sella, etc.; Engl. sit, seat] , to sit.此词可以在法语，西班牙语里得到验证


euge (euge or eugae , Ter. And. 2, 2, 8), interj., = euge, an exclamation of joy,
 applause, admiration, etc., well done! good! bravo!


sophos , adv., = sophos, an exclamation of applause, well done! well said! bravo!其实可以从英语Philosophy(philo=love;sophy=wisdom)得到部分理解，好像德国最出名的软件公司叫SOPHOS
